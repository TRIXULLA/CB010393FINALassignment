SRI LANKAN WILD LIFE

Sri Lanka is abundant in wildlife. The country's national parks are home to a diverse range of amazing creatures, including herds of trumpeting Asian elephants, elusive leopards, and strange-looking sloth bears. Here's a rundown of what to expect during your visit

YALA

It's an exciting adventure that gets you up up and personal with the wild world of nature. Whether you are an avid nature lover or visiting a wildlife park for the first time, if you come with an open mind and a want to explore and learn, you will leave with a fresh perspective on life. Yala is a rigorous nature reserve combined with a national park. The park, which is divided into five blocks, protects about 130,000 hectares of terrain that includes light woods, scrubs, grasslands, tanks, and lagoons. Currently, two blocks are available to the public. Yala was named a wildlife sanctuary in 1900 and a national park in 1938. It is located in Sri Lanka's south-east, bordering the magnificent Indian Ocean.

WILPATTU

Wilpattu Sanctuary was designated as a National Park in 1938. It is located on the west coast, near the historical city of Anuradhapura.The jungle in the dry zone is densely wooded. Wilpattu National Park contains many villus, or natural lakes that dot the landscape. With the exception of two, these lakes contain rainfall and are thus important for both resident and migratory waterbirds.

UDAWALAWE NATIONAL PARK

Udawalawe National Park in Sri Lanka is located on the border of the provinces of Sabaragamuwa and Uva. The park was established to provide a safe haven for wild creatures displaced as a result of the construction of the Udawalawe Reservoir on the Walawe River, as well as to safeguard the reservoir's catchment area.
